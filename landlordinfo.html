    Non-Resident Landlords (NRL)

    Landlords who reside outside the UK will need to check with the Inland Revenue & Customs (HMRC) whether or not you are classified as a Non-Resident Landlord (NRL).

    HMRC regard Non-Resident Landlords as person(s) whose usual home is outside the UK and whom have a rental property in the UK.

    If you are a Non-Resident Landlord, it is very important that you contact HMRC, discuss your circumstances and follow their advice. They may grant approval for no tax to be deducted from your rental income. If this is the case, you would need to ensure that they write to us confirming the decision. If you are not living in the UK and are abroad for 6 months or more, MQ Lettings are required to hold 20% of your monthly rent and pass this on Quarterly to HMRC.

    To avoid this, you as an individual, can fill out an NRL1i form and the full rent can continue being paid to yourself. You will need our NA code is NA 056316 This is the link

    Landlord Registration Scotland (every three years)

    As of April 2006, all landlords in Scotland must register themselves with Landlord Registration Scotland. Once you are registered, you must ensure that all your rented properties are linked to your relevant landlord registration account(s).

    Likewise, all Scottish letting agents must also be registered. You will therefore need to add our registration number to your live application(s). This is a legal requirement and can be done online at: Our Scottish Agent Registration Number is LARN1902051 which you should add during the registration process.

    Contents and Buildings Insurance

    You will need to ensure that your rental property has the appropriate landlord’s building and contents insurance. If your property is within a factored development which has a block insurance policy, you will need to contact your factors and advise them that this particular property is rented. They will then update the block insurance policy.

    Length of Tenancy

    A Scottish Private Residential Tenancy Agreement SPRTA is proposed. draft copy attached. The SPRTA has no set length of Tenancy period. Period of notice has been affected by Coronavirus (COVID-19) Private Residential Tenancies

    In normal circumstances as a very general summary- The Tenant is required to provide 28 days noticeto end the Agreement at any time during the Tenancy Period.

    The landlord is required to provide 28 days notice to end the Agreement during the first six month Tenancy Period.

    The landlord is required to provide 84 days notice to end the Agreement following the initial six month Tenancy Period.

    In providing the notice, the landlord is required to provide one or more of the Statutory Eviction Ground (or Grounds) for Eviction. The Grounds provided may alter the Notice Period required by the landlord.

    Repairing Standard

    Landlords in the private rented sector have a duty to ensure that a rented house meets a basic standard of repair called the “repairing standard”. If a rented house does not meet that standard, and the landlord delays or refuses to carry out the necessary repairs, the tenant can apply for a decision by a Private Rented Housing Committee (a “Committee”) on whether or not the landlord has failed to comply with that duty. The Committee can then order the landlord to carry out the necessary repairs. Various penalties apply if the landlord then does not do so. Further information can be found at

    Safety and Legal Recommendations & Requirements

    The following are legal requirements or recommendations (as indicated) You are welcome to make your own arrangements as long as we are provided with proof of compliance. Unless we receive word in writing to the contrary, we shall arrange for the following to be completed for your property.

    Energy Performance certificate - EPC

    We shall arrange for an Energy Performance Certificate. This is a legal requirement. A property can only be let if it has an EPC rating of E or above. The EPC is valid for 10 years Completion of Energy Performance Certificate – EPC £90 inclusive of VAT.

    Legionnaires' Disease Risk Assessment

    Landlords of residential accommodation have a responsibility to carry out risk assessments for Legionella bacteria and maintain control measures to minimise the risk of Legionnaires’ disease. We recommend the Legionnaires’ Disease Risk Assessment is completed every two years. Complete a Legionnaires Disease Risk Assessment £90 inclusive of VAT.

    Gas Safety Certificate

    We shall arrange for a Landlord's Gas Safety Certificate to be carried out. This is a legal requirement. The Landlord's Gas Safety Certificate Inspection will then be required to be carried out annually. Complete a Gas Safe Check £90 inclusive of VAT. (Normally only one charge per property -depending on type of appliances)

    Electrical Safety

    We shall arrange for an Electrical Installation Condition Report to be carried out. This is a legal requirement. An Electrical Installation Condition Report is thereafter required every five years Complete an Electrical Installation Condition Report. -£140 inclusive of VAT.

    We shall arrange for a Landlord's Portable Appliance Test PAT to be carried out. This is a legal requirement. We recommend the Portable Appliance Test is then carried out annually. Complete a Portable Appliance Test £90 inclusive of VAT.

    Please note -Combined cost of an Electrical Installation Condition Report. EICR and Portable Appliance Test. PAT -£170 inclusive of VAT.

    Smoke and Heat Alarms

    The Scottish Repairing Standard requires all rented property to be fitted with mains powered smoke detectors in all circulation areas/hallways and also a smoke detector in the living room and a heat detector in the kitchen. All alarms must be interlinked. This is a legal requirement.

    Notes: Our electricians will complete work by means of concealing cables within the fabric of the property where practical and by use of conduit/trunking. Electricians will endeavour to incorporate any existing alarms into the new system. If you wish to further preserve the aesthetics of the internal features it may be appropriate to use wireless technology to interlink the alarms due to the property lay out or décor. This will incur additional costs due to the increased costs of equipment. If you wish such an installation, please make this clear in your instruction.

    This will incur additional costs due to the increased costs of equipment (approx. £40 inclusive of VAT. per extra alarm) Supply and installation of each Mains Powered Smoke/Heat Alarm - £115 inclusive of VAT.

    Carbon Monoxide Detectors

    Under requirements introduced by the Housing (Scotland) Act 2014, Properties with fossil fuel appliances and/or boilers must have carbon monoxide (CO) detectors fitted in their properties. This is a legal requirement. Supply and install carbon monoxide (CO) detectors £50 inclusive of VAT.

    Fire Blanket and/ or Extinguisher

    A fire blanket and/or extinguisher should be provided in the kitchen and should be maintained and serviced regularly. This is a recommendation Supply and installation of a Fire Blanket and 2Kg Powder Fire Extinguisher -£60 inclusive of VAT.

    Drain Clearing Equipment

    Many call outs are for blocked drains leaks or faulty heating systems. Often these are minor matters that can be addressed by Tenants, of they have the correct equipment. We recommend the provision of a sink plunger, a toilet plunger, a bucket and a radiator bleed key. Supply of the above equipment £40 inclusive of VAT per property.

    Safety Clips for Blinds Cords

    Safety clips should be installed on all blinds to reduce choking hazard for children. This is a recommendation Supply and installation of Safety clips £40 inclusive of VAT per property.

    Furniture Compliance

    Please confirm that all furniture in the property was purchased new after 1988 and therefore meets the fire safety regulations. All older furniture not meeting these regulations should be removed. Please see:This is a Legal Requirement.
